Winter Storm in Idaho
======

The national weather service forecasts heavy, wet snow to be expected in Lemhi
County above 6,500 feet (1980 m). The high temperature will drop 20 degrees (F)
to 37 F, and lows will drop to 33 F. All brought by the extreme cold air behind
a strong cold front featured by a tropopause polar vortex to its south. Of
course the heavy snowfall can only happen in a mountain area with elevated
height.

This reminds me of a snowfall event in early June (Jun 11) in Na'qu., Xinjiang.
The 24-h total precipitation was 28.4 mm. The elevation of that Na'qu is 4500 m.

Also in Idaho, Boise was hit by [a severe hail event](https://www.youtube.com/shorts/ptP_MJvIFPI), with a total of 0.34 inches
of precipitation on Jun 27.

The weather map forecasts critical fire weather for the Four Corners, severe
thunderstorm in Dakotas ahead of the occluded front. On the east coast, flash
flooding should be watched out for in Kentucy and North Carolina ahead of a
stationary front and dry line. Meanwhile extrem heat advisories have been issued
for upper Midwest and in Maryland that are in the control of the high pressure
system.

Meiyu front precipitation
======

I moved to a new apartment this weekend. Thank god (Typhoon Mekkehla, really, for
sucking the moisture towards itself) that the weather is fine during the weekend. But
the Meiyu stationary front will be back again to govern the lower Yangtze River
and bring a week-long precipitation in Shanghai. Heavy precipitation of 250-400
mm over the next week on the board of An'hui and Zhe'jiang provinces.

Over the past week, heavy precipitation was mostly over souther China, in
Yun'nan, Guang'xi, Guang'dong, souther Fujian Province, and over Taiwan. Southern Taiwan
had over 880 mm rainfall in 24 hours on Jun 25. The cause was the weak
southweasterly and the over-night land wind formed a lower-level convergence
that excited strong convection to move over land.

Chen Nuo (陈诺)

Atmospheric Scientist · University of Oklahoma

I am an atmospheric scientist and postdoctoral researcher in the AAARG group at the University of Oklahoma. My research explores the interactions between summertime Arctic cyclones, tropopause polar vortices (TPVs), and Arctic sea ice using the Community Earth System Model (CESM2). I also specialize in numerical modeling, data assimilation, and the integration of machine learning in atmospheric science.

Featured Research

1

Summertime Arctic Cyclone & Sea Ice Coupling

Assessing the physical interactions and feedbacks between Tropopause Polar Vortices (TPVs), Arctic Cyclones (ACs), and sea ice cover using fully coupled Community Earth System Model version 2 (CESM2) simulations.

CESM2 Arctic Cyclones TPV Sea Ice Coupling
2

Pangu-DART Interface for Data Assimilation

Developing an interface between machine learning numerical weather prediction models (Pangu-Weather) and NCAR's Data Assimilation Research Testbed (DART) to successfully ingest atmospheric observations into ML-based forecast ensembles.

Data Assimilation DART Pangu-Weather Machine Learning
3

Balanced Adjoint PV Sensitivity

Formulating geostrophically balanced initial sensitivities to temperature and wind fields using Quasi-Geostrophic Potential Vorticity (QGPV), which filters out unphysical gravity waves in the WRF adjoint model.

Adjoint Sensitivity QGPV WRF Adjoint Gravity Waves
